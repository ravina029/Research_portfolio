---

## 🧠 Research Identity

My research is centered on **building trustworthy and interpretable NLP systems**, with a particular focus on **reliability, safety, and evidence-grounded reasoning in Large Language Models (LLMs)**.

I am interested in addressing core limitations of modern NLP systems, including:
- hallucination and lack of faithfulness in generated outputs  
- absence of calibrated uncertainty in high-stakes settings  
- limited transparency in model decision-making  

My work approaches these challenges through:
- **retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)** for grounding outputs in external evidence  
- **explainability methods** (LIME, SHAP) to analyze model behavior  
- **evaluation frameworks** that go beyond accuracy to measure faithfulness, safety, and robustness  

A key direction in my research is the design of **verification-aware NLP pipelines**, where model outputs are explicitly validated using:
- retrieval alignment  
- contradiction detection  
- safety-aware filtering  

Through my work, I aim to develop NLP systems that are:
- **auditable**  
- **interpretable**  
- **reliable in real-world, high-stakes environments**  

**Long-term goal:**  
To contribute to the development of **trustworthy, transparent, and verifiable AI systems** suitable for real-world deployment.
